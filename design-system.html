Principles

Split Brain is built on one idea: my work comes from two halves of one brain. Navy is the technical and product side. Rust is the creative and advertising side. Every color, card, and button on the site picks a side, and this page documents only what the site actually uses, nothing aspirational.

Or at least I hope...

Honest by default

Every token and component here is pulled from the live site. If it isn't used, it isn't shown.

Two minds, one whole

Navy and rust are equal and opposite — two views of the same mind. Category color signals which kind of work you're looking at.

Accessible on purpose

Focus states, skip links, AA-contrast text, and reduced-motion support are baked in, not bolted on.

Spacing & Scale

Type Scale

Sizes follow a Major Third scale (each step is 1.25× the last), built on a 16px base. This keeps headings and body text in a consistent rhythm.

H1 · 32px · 2rem
H2 · 28px · 1.75rem
H3 · 24px · 1.5rem
H4 · 20px · 1.25rem
Body · 16px · 1rem
Small · 14px · 0.875rem

Corner Radius

Three rounding roles do most of the work: a gentle radius for small surfaces, a larger one for cards, and a full pill for badges and buttons.

Motion & Interactions

Tech Stack Marquee

A row of tool icons that scrolls on its own and loops forever. Hover any icon to see its name, or hover the row to pause it.

Respects prefers-reduced-motion — the scroll stops for visitors who ask their system to reduce motion.

Live Clocks

Real-time clocks in the footer, showing the current location and Tokyo, each paired with a day/night visual (a sun by day, the live moon phase by night).

Beside each clock sits a sun by day or the moon's current phase by night. The phase is calculated live from the lunar cycle, so it always matches the real sky.

Hover to Translate

Japanese words show their English meaning when you hover over them. A nod to my study abroad trip that doesn't alienate anyone who can't read it. Rendered in Noto Sans JP.

Copy Email Link

Click to copy my email to your clipboard; keep clicking and the confirmation messages get sillier. I'm not a fan of links that hijack your mail app, so I went with copy and paste instead.

Icons

Small UI icons used throughout the site: carousel arrows, the info marker, the external-link arrow, and the two Split Brain logo halves. All are SVGs, so they stay crisp at any size and recolor to match their surroundings.
